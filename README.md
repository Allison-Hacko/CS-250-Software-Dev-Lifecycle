Reflection

Throughout this course, I learned how to better understand user needs and turn them into actual program features. Interpreting user needs means looking at what the user is trying to accomplish and breaking that down into smaller, clear requirements. Creating user stories really helps with this because they focus on the user’s perspective, usually in a format like “As a user, I want ___ so that ___.” This makes it easier to stay focused on what actually matters instead of just building random features. It also helps prioritize work and keep development organized.

When it comes to developing programs, I’ve learned to approach things step by step instead of trying to do everything at once. Agile has shown me the importance of working in small increments, getting feedback, and improving over time. In the future, I definitely want to keep using Agile processes like sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives. These help keep work structured while still being flexible. Also, being able to adapt quickly instead of stressing over making everything perfect the first time is honestly a game changer.

Being a good team member in software development means more than just writing code. It involves communication, accountability, and being willing to help others. A strong team member listens to feedback, shares ideas, and stays open to change. It also means actually showing up and doing your part. Agile really emphasizes teamwork, and I’ve learned that collaboration is just as important as technical skills.

Overall, this course helped me understand how Agile and Scrum work in real-world development. I feel more confident in working on teams, organizing tasks, and building projects in a more structured and efficient way.
